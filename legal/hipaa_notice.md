## Notice of Privacy Practices for Protected Health Information (PHI)

This Notice describes how medical and health information about you may be used and disclosed by Biohacker Systems ("we," "our," or "us") and how you can access this information.

**PLEASE REVIEW THIS NOTICE CAREFULLY.**

## 1. Our Legal Duty

We are required by law to:
- Maintain the privacy of your Protected Health Information (PHI)
- Provide you with this Notice of our legal duties and privacy practices
- Follow the terms of the Notice currently in effect
- Notify you in the event of a breach of your unsecured PHI

---

## 2. What is Protected Health Information (PHI)?

PHI is information that identifies you and relates to:
- Your past, present, or future physical or mental health
- Healthcare services provided to you
- Payment for healthcare services

**In the Biohacker App, PHI includes:**
- Laboratory test results and biomarker data
- Peptide protocols and dosing schedules
- Cycle tracking information
- Health metrics and trends
- Research notes related to your health
- Any information that could identify you in combination with health data

---

## 3. How We May Use and Disclose Your PHI

### 3.1 Uses and Disclosures with Your Authorization

We will obtain your written authorization before using or disclosing your PHI except as described below. You may revoke your authorization at any time by contacting privacy@biohacker.systems.

### 3.2 Uses and Disclosures Without Your Authorization

We may use or disclose your PHI without your authorization in the following circumstances:

#### A. Treatment
We do **not** provide treatment, but we may disclose your PHI to healthcare providers involved in your care if you choose to export or share your data with them.

#### B. Payment
We do **not** currently bill for services, but if subscription fees are introduced, we may use your PHI to process payments and verify coverage.

#### C. Healthcare Operations
We may use PHI for:
- Quality improvement initiatives
- Security audits and monitoring
- Training staff on privacy practices
- Improving App functionality (de-identified data only)

#### D. Required by Law
We must disclose PHI when required by:
- Federal, state, or local law
- Court orders or subpoenas
- Law enforcement investigations
- Public health authorities (e.g., disease reporting)
- Health oversight agencies (e.g., HHS audits)

#### E. Public Health Activities
We may disclose PHI to public health authorities for:
- Disease prevention or control
- Reporting adverse events or product defects
- FDA-regulated product surveillance

#### F. Law Enforcement
We may disclose PHI to law enforcement when:
- Required by court order or subpoena
- Identifying or locating a suspect or fugitive
- Reporting a crime on our premises
- Responding to emergencies

#### G. To Avert Serious Threat
We may disclose PHI if necessary to prevent or lessen a serious and imminent threat to:
- Your health or safety
- The health or safety of others
- Public safety

#### H. Business Associates
We may disclose PHI to third-party service providers ("Business Associates") who perform services on our behalf, such as:
- **Supabase:** Database and authentication services
- **Google Cloud Platform:** Backend AI analysis services
- **Analytics Providers:** De-identified usage data only (no PHI)

All Business Associates are required to sign Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) committing to HIPAA compliance.

## 4. Your Rights Regarding Your PHI

You have the following rights regarding your PHI:

### 4.1 Right to Access
You have the right to inspect and obtain a copy of your PHI stored in the App.

**How to Exercise:**
- Use the "Export Data" feature in the App
- Request via email: privacy@biohacker.systems
- We will provide your data within **30 days** of your request

**Format:** JSON, CSV, or PDF (your choice)  
**Fee:** No fee for electronic copies; reasonable fees may apply for paper copies

### 4.2 Right to Amend
You have the right to request amendments to your PHI if you believe it is incorrect or incomplete.

**How to Exercise:**
- Edit directly in the App
- Email requests to: privacy@biohacker.systems

**Response Time:** Within 60 days  
**Denial:** We may deny your request if the information is accurate and complete. You have the right to submit a statement of disagreement.

### 4.3 Right to an Accounting of Disclosures
You have the right to receive a list of disclosures we have made of your PHI in the past 6 years (excluding disclosures for treatment, payment, or healthcare operations).

**How to Exercise:** Email privacy@biohacker.systems  
**Response Time:** Within 60 days  
**Fee:** First request free; reasonable fees for subsequent requests within 12 months

### 4.4 Right to Request Restrictions
You have the right to request restrictions on how we use or disclose your PHI.

**How to Exercise:** Email privacy@biohacker.systems  
**Note:** We are not required to agree to your request, except in limited circumstances (e.g., disclosure to a health plan if you paid out-of-pocket in full).

### 4.5 Right to Request Confidential Communications
You have the right to request that we communicate with you about your PHI by alternative means or at alternative locations.

**How to Exercise:** Email privacy@biohacker.systems with your preferred contact method  
**Approval:** We will accommodate reasonable requests without requiring an explanation

### 4.6 Right to a Paper Copy of This Notice
You have the right to request a paper copy of this Notice at any time.

**How to Exercise:** Email privacy@biohacker.systems or download from the App

### 4.7 Right to Notification of a Breach
You have the right to be notified in the event of a breach of your unsecured PHI.

**Notification Timeframe:** Within 60 days of discovery  
**Method:** Email, in-app notification, and/or mail (if email unavailable)

## 5. Security Safeguards

We implement administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to protect your PHI:

### 5.1 Administrative Safeguards
- HIPAA privacy and security training for all employees
- Designated Privacy Officer and Security Officer
- Risk assessments and security audits
- Incident response procedures
- Business Associate Agreements with all third parties

### 5.2 Physical Safeguards
- Secure data centers with restricted access (Supabase/GCP)
- Redundant backups in geographically distributed locations
- Environmental controls (fire suppression, climate control)

### 5.3 Technical Safeguards
- **Encryption at Rest:** AES-256 for all PHI stored in the database
- **Encryption in Transit:** TLS 1.3 for all network communications
- **Authentication:** Secure password hashing (bcrypt) + biometric authentication
- **Access Controls:** Row-level security in Supabase; principle of least privilege
- **Session Management:** Automatic timeout after 15 minutes of inactivity
- **Audit Logging:** All access to PHI is logged and monitored
- **Intrusion Detection:** Automated monitoring for suspicious activity

---

## 6. Breach Notification Procedures

In the event of a breach of your unsecured PHI:

### 6.1 Our Obligations
We will:
- Investigate the breach within 24 hours of discovery
- Notify you within **60 days** of discovering the breach
- Notify the Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) as required
- Notify media outlets if breach affects 500+ individuals in a jurisdiction
- Provide details including: what happened, what data was involved, steps we're taking, what you can do

### 6.2 Your Actions
If you believe your PHI has been compromised:
- Contact us immediately: security@biohacker.systems
- Monitor your accounts for suspicious activity
- Consider credit monitoring if financial data was involved
